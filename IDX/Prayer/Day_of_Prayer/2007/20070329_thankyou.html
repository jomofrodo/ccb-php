Day of Prayer for Burma 2007 Inside Karen State

March 2007

Dear friends,

Thank you for praying for the people of Burma, especially on the Global Day of Prayer for Burma on March 11, 2007. People inside Burma also prayed and here are pictures of one group of Karen displaced people who along with a Karen youth organization, gathered together in their village to pray.
 


Before the prayer service, the team passed out balloons for the children and put a large banner up, which read, "Day of Prayer for Burma March 11, 2007."

It was a beautiful scene of people singing together on a hilltop with a gentle breeze blowing

and tall mountains all around.

The people prayed and we all thanked God that people all over the world were praying too. Even though troubles may come and the oppressors seem so strong, God will help us to endure, to overcome and to live each day with hope and joy.

Earlier the same day we met with a family that told us about the attempted rape of their 13-year-old niece by the Burma Army. The village they live in is very close to a Burma Army camp and they are visited regularly by Burma Army soldiers.

They told us that two weeks earlier a Burma Army soldier tried to rape three women in one night. The first resisted him so he threw his knife at her. She was struck deeply in the ankle but managed to run away. The soldier then went to another house and entered it and grabbed a woman who was there with her baby. She broke free of his grasp and ran away. He picked up her baby and told her, "I have your baby, you must come back." The baby began to cry so the soldier put the baby down and went to a third house. Inside the house he found a 13-year-old girl who was sleeping. He tried to rape her but she woke up and began kicking and calling for help. Her mother and father, who were next door, and her aunt and uncle (the aunt was the woman who had been wounded earlier by the thrown knife), came to help her and arrived before the soldier could rape her. When confronted by the family, the soldier ran away. The villagers were all afraid that they would be attacked for resisting the Burma Army and said they were angry that they could not approach the Burma Army commander about this issue.

Her family said, "We are very happy you all came and that we could tell you what happened. It is very dangerous here and we do not know what the Burma Army will do next. We want the world to know our situation. May God bless you, please come again."

The girl said, "Thank you for coming and for me, I am well."

We were happy that the girl had not been raped but also sad for these people who had to live under constant threat. We walked back to the place where we would join in the day of prayer and I thought this is one of the many reasons why we pray for change in Burma. And as we prayed I was reminded that we have hope because no matter what happens in this world, God has prepared a place for us. This also means we can choose to do the right thing even if that means losing, because our lives are eternal with God.


 

Thank you all for your prayers, love and support for the people of Burma.


Christians Concerned for Burma
March 2007
