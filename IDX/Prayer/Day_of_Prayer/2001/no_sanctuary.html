Life Without Sanctuary

Karen IDP man observes what remains of his IDP site that the Burmese military burned to the ground. Picture by FBR

" In the time of trouble He shall hide me in His pavilion; In the secret place of his tabernacle he shall hide me; He shall set me high upon a rock.". Psalm 27:5

  The ethnic people of Burma as well as the Burman people continue their struggle for basic human rights, democracy, and freedom. Dictators have ruled Burma since 1962 using force to relocate entire communities and implementing a brutal . four cuts. strategy to silence all dissent. Since the bloody crushing of the 1988 democracy movement led by Aung San Suu Kyi, the Burma Army has intensified its attacks on all the people, but especially the ethnic groups who still resist their rule. Due to the oppression of the dictators of Burma there are approximately 2 million people inside Burma who have been displaced from their homes. For most displaced ethnic Burmans, approximately 1 million, relocation has been by force in order to make more land available for the regime. s business projects. These displaced Burmans live in or near large urban centers firmly under the regime. s control.

For the ethnic minorities who comprise over 40 percent of Burma. s population, the situation is much worse. In the minority areas such as the Karen, Karenni, and Shan states, up to 1 million people have been forced to leave their homes with only what they can carry and live in relocation sites where their freedom is severely restricted and their treatment brutal. In the Karen and Karenni states alone, over 200,000 are languishing in these sites. The villagers. livestock are usually confiscated and their villages are burned to the ground. Many areas are then mined to prevent villagers from returning. In some cases the villagers are not allowed to go to a relocation site, but are attacked by the Burma Army, murdered, raped, chased into the jungle along with having their villages burned down.

The regime. s stated purpose is to "crush all resistance" and to "punish" the ethnic groups. The result of this scorched-earth policy is that many have fled their homes or the brutal conditions of the relocation sites. These people (over 400,000 in Karen and Karenni States) are currently hiding in the jungle along the Thai-Burma border. They are in need of security, medical care, clothing, school supplies, moral encouragement and spiritual support.

Pictured on this page is a burnt IDP settlement that used to be home to 4,000 people. The site was attacked and destroyed by the Burma Army on April 4, 2000. For many who have fled this place, it is not the first time they have had their houses burned and their communities destroyed. Pray for an end to the terrible destruction.
