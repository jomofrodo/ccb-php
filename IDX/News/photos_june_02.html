June 2002 Photographs

Courtesy Free Burma Rangers

  • June 10, 2002: Internally Displaced Karen family hiding in the jungle after their home was destroyed by the Burma Army.
  • June 13, 2002 : 6yr. old survivor of Htee Law Bleh massacre. He was shot in the arm by Burma Army soldiers of LIB 78. He pretended to be dead and later escaped walking 3 days alone to safety. Twelve karen villagers were killed in this massacre on April 28, 2002.
  • June 17, 2002: Villagers stand in the ruins of a church. The Burma Army burned this church down along with 5 others and 6 villages in Dooplaya District, Karen State.
  • June 18, 2002: Internally Displaced Persons fleeing attacks of the Burma Army LIB 301 and 78 in Dooplaya District, Karen State. These Karen villagers are in a group of 96 (of the 5,000 displaced in this area) who are attempting to flee attacks of forced relocation by the Burma Army. They walked for 10 days with the elderly, newborns, and sick making their progress slow and dangerous. The Burma Army shelled and chased and attempted to ambush them as they fled to Thailand.
