U.S. Senators urge Bush to promote democracy in Myanmar
 Tue Oct 7, 5:26 PM ET
WASHINGTON (AFP) - Thirty-five US senators urged President George W. Bush
 (news - web sites) to use his upcoming trip to Thailand to further the  cause of democracy in Myanmar, which they say has become a serious threat to
 regional security.


"The United States and all its allies -- particularly Thailand -- must
sustain a full court press on the repressive junta in Rangoon to secure
the immediate and unconditional release of Burmese democracy leader Daw Aung  San Suu Kyi and her colleagues," said Republican Senator Mitch McConnell of
Kentucky, referring to Myanmar by its former name Burma.

 

 The petition said that Myanmar's refusal to confront an AIDS (news - web sites) crisis is
devastating to the health systems of neighboring countries, while internal repressions were saddling them with millions of refugees. Democratic Senator Dianne Feinstein of California said the current situation should be unacceptable not only to Bush but also to countries in the region, especially Thailand. "The status quo means only continued suffering for the people of Burma," Feinstein said. "Change will only come to that country with the help of the international community, including our regional partners." The United States and some other nations tightened sanctions on Myanmar after Aung San Suu Kyi was detained at an undisclosed location following a bloody clash between her supporters and pro-junta demonstrators on May 30. The Nobel peace laureate was admitted to the hospital on September 17 for surgery and transferred from there to house arrest on September 26. It is her third house arrest since the late 1980s but the
junta is portraying her current status as a positive and planned development.
