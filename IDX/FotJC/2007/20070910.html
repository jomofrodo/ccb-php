FLIGHT OF THE JUNGLE CHICKEN

    
  September 10, 2007    

 

Dear friends,

We are back in Thailand and getting ready to go into the field for continuing missions. We were overwhelmed by the love you all gave us and the support - spiritual, moral, and material.


Thanks for all the good talks, walks, runs, hunts, mountain climbs, horse, boat, bike and plane rides and the opportunities to enjoy you all and the nature of America, Denmark and Norway. We are glad to be back but as we boarded the plane felt like we were leaving a pieces of our heart with you there.

We are so glad to have such a wonderful family and team in you all and now before we go back inside we want to share the things we learned on this trip.

First are some of the many lessons we learned from you while in the USA:

1) Our country is full of wonderful people from coast to coast and many people in our Government are dedicated, warm hearted, clear thinking people who do their best to serve and need our prayers.
2) The Church is a great light of truth, mercy, love and hope for our country and the world.
3) We want to be part of the Kingdom of God that is everywhere in the world and shines in the darkest places. We do not want to be about building our own Kingdoms or looking for "market share". We want to be about building God's Kingdom. Then we are free to enjoy this life and be useful for God.
4) We want to forgive and bless our enemies. On this trip a friend- Doug Yoder, asked me, "When Pharaoh sent out the edict to kill all the Jewish boys, who saved Moses?" ... then he said, "Pharaoh's daughter saved Moses. the daughter of the enemy." It reminded me that all people are God's children and God works through even our enemies. Nothing is higher than His purpose or evil enough to overcome God's ability to bring good from all circumstances. When we ask God to bless our enemies we are freed form the burden of offense, resentment and worry. I know for me too, one of the first ways that God has blessed me in my own life is to show me my own sin.
5) And from Franklin Graham, who I met on a dirt airstrip in Alaska - "God likes it when we talk well about His Son...always work for the eternal and pray that all your team members follow Jesus"
.
Lessons we learned in Burma and wanted to share this time in America:

1) We want to thank God for all things at all times and see what God does.
2) We don't want to make any decision based on comfort or fear.
3) When making a decision as a group, we try to offer up all opinions to God and commit to obey God no matter the result. When we do that we find we are led in the same way.
4) We do not serve a cause, we serve our Lord Jesus. I was reminded and convicted of this by our chief Karen medic Eliya Samson.


For these and many other lessons and encouragements that each of you gave us, thank you. May God bless us all and lead us back together again.


We love and pray for you,


Dave, Karen, Sahale, Suuzanne, Peter Eliya and all of the team here.
